First aid is immediate help given to someone with an injury or sudden illness until professional help is available.

For minor cuts, wash hands, rinse the wound with clean water, apply gentle pressure to stop bleeding, use a clean bandage, and watch for signs of infection such as increasing redness, swelling, warmth, pus, or fever.

For burns, cool the burn under cool running water for about 20 minutes if possible. Do not apply ice directly. Cover the area with a clean non-stick dressing. Seek medical care for large, deep, chemical, electrical, facial, hand, foot, genital, or joint burns.

For choking in an adult who cannot cough, speak, or breathe, call emergency services and begin appropriate first aid such as abdominal thrusts if trained to do so.

Call emergency services immediately for severe bleeding, trouble breathing, chest pain, stroke symptoms, loss of consciousness, severe allergic reaction, major burns, or serious injury.
