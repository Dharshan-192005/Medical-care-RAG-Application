Hypertension means blood pressure is consistently higher than recommended. It often has no symptoms, so regular measurement is important.

Lifestyle steps that can support healthy blood pressure include limiting sodium, eating fruits and vegetables, being physically active, maintaining a healthy weight, limiting alcohol, avoiding tobacco, managing stress, and sleeping well.

Healthcare professionals may prescribe medicines for hypertension. People should not stop or change prescribed blood pressure medication without speaking with their clinician.

Home blood pressure readings can help clinicians understand patterns over time. A proper cuff size, seated posture, back support, feet flat on the floor, and resting quietly before measuring can improve accuracy.

Seek emergency care for chest pain, severe shortness of breath, sudden weakness or numbness, sudden severe headache, confusion, fainting, or vision changes.
